Revenue Model – Streams & Pricing

Multi-stream monetisation designed to keep delivery fees ultra-low

LocalLook monetises across 4 revenue streams—capturing value from boutiques and customers while using low delivery fees (₹0–₹30) to drive volume and habit formation.

1) Commission on Orders

Primary revenue stream (paid by boutique)

Base range: 12%–18% of order value (deducted from boutique payout).

Tier-based: Tier-1 15%–18% (higher AOV, stronger demand) • Tier-2/3 12%–15% (adoption-led).

Onboarding promo (first 3 months): Tier-1 10% • Tier-2/3 8%.

2) Delivery Fee (Customer Paid)

Dynamic pricing to enable high frequency

Range: ₹0–₹30 (capped), dynamically set by distance, real-time demand and order value.

Distance bands: <2 km Free • 2–5 km ₹15–₹20 • >5 km ₹25–₹30.

Order-value trigger: orders > ₹2,500 get free delivery (per plan).

3) Featured Listings (Boutique Subscription)

Paid visibility + performance insights

Pricing: ₹2,000–₹5,000 / month (varies by tier and placement).

What boutiques get: top-of-search placement and category prominence to drive higher conversion.

Premium tier: includes analytics dashboard + performance insights to improve assortment and pricing.

4) Festival Campaign Fees

Co-marketing + dedicated capacity during peaks

Event fee: ₹3,000–₹5,000 / event (as per plan ranges; varies by city tier).

Incremental take-rate: +2%–3% commission on campaign-driven sales.

Deliverables: in-app banners, social promotion and dedicated rider capacity during festive spikes (Diwali, Durga Puja, Eid, etc.).

Note: Delivery fee is intentionally kept low to drive volume; contribution margins improve with rider utilisation and optimised routing at scale.

STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT

SWOT Analysis

Internal & External Factors

Strengths

  • First Mover Advantage: Only player offering sub-60-minute fashion delivery in Tier-1 & 2 cities.
  • Asset-Light Model: Zero inventory holding; highly scalable with minimal capital expenditure.
  • Ultra-Low Delivery Fee: Dynamic ₹0-30 fee structure removes price barrier, driving volume.
  • Exclusive Partnerships: Tie-ups with top local boutiques create a supply-side moat.

Weaknesses

  • Tech Dependency: Reliant on real-time inventory sync accuracy from partner stores.
  • Unit Economics Pressure: Thin margins on delivery fees require high order density to cover rider costs.
  • Brand Awareness: New entrant requiring significant initial marketing spend for trust.

Opportunities

  • Market White Space: Untapped demand for instant fashion across Tier-1 and Tier-2/3 cities.
  • Tech Innovations: AR virtual try-on and AI-driven demand forecasting for partners.
  • Subscription Model: Recurring revenue through "LocalLook Gold" for free delivery.
  • EV Integration: Lowering long-term logistics costs with electric vehicle fleet.

Threats

  • Big Player Entry: Zepto, Blinkit, or Swiggy expanding from grocery into fashion.
  • Regulatory Changes: Evolving labor codes regarding gig economy workers (riders).
  • Urban Congestion: Traffic in metros impacting the 60-minute delivery guarantee.
